Tagging
    Relevant OSM tags:
        - https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Dstone
          A large (whatever that might mean) natural boulder or rock fragment that is not attached to the underlying terrain.
        - https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Drock
          A notable rock feature or small group of rocks, attached to the underlying bedrock, mostly used on a single node element.
        - https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Dbare_rock
          An area of bare rock is sparsely vegetated or not vegetated at all, so that the solid bedrock becomes visible.
        - https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Dblockfield
          A surface covered by boulder- or block-sized rocks. Blockfields differ from screes and talus slope in that blockfields do not apparently originate from mass wastings.
        - https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Dscree
          Scree is an accumulation of unconsolidated angular stones. Scree is formed by rockfall from adjacent rock faces
          and often accumulates in the form of sloped cones called talus. Scree consists of rock fragments with sharp edges
          that can vary in size from millimetres to meters.
